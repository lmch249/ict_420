Differences in Engines
V4 Engine
A type of engine with four cylinders arranged in a "V" shape, meaning the cylinders are split into two banks with a V-angle between them, allowing for a more compact design compared to an inline-four engine while still delivering more power (source: Wikipedia article V4 Engine)
V6 Engine
A V6 engine is a six-cylinder piston engine where the cylinders and cylinder blocks share a common crankshaft and are arranged in a V configuration. (source: Wikipedia article V4 Engine)
V8 Engine
A V8 engine is an eight-cylinder piston engine in which two banks of four cylinders share a common crankshaft and are arranged in a V configuration. (source: Wikipedia article V4 Engine)
